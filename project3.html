Bottle Opener Redesign

Skills: Prototyping | Interview Methodology | Collaboration


Written Report

This project emphasized the importance of the Double Diamond Process, where there would be divergence to discover problems and ideas as well as convergence to refine and narrow the scope of the problem. Bottle openers can try to include several features within a compact figure in an attempt to satisfy users, but I discovered that these bottle openers usually design for one type of user: strong men. Furthermore, bottle openers can lack feedback, discoverability, and signifiers, which is ultimately frustrating for users because their mental model of how bottle openers work does not match the bottle openers' poor system image and conceptual model.

My design team's solution to this problem was to design a bottle opener that clearly signifies the affordances of the product and does not have systematic bias towards certain users. Further detail can be seen in the full written report!
